Marketing

Today's strategies to go viral

Format

"Process reveal" reels are outperforming polished product shots across Instagram right now — showing the making-of (a cake being iced, a brass showpiece being cast, a client brief turning into a moodboard) gets 2-3x the saves of a static final-product post. Every brand below has a natural process to show.

Hook

Open on the objection, not the offer. "Why your Instagram looks the same as everyone else's" outperforms "Check out our new package" as a first line — say the problem the viewer already has before you say what you sell.

Distribution

Post once, cut three ways. Same footage → 60s Reel (hook-led), 3-slide carousel (quotable line as slide 1), and a Story poll/question sticker to farm replies into DMs. Stop treating platforms as separate content jobs.

Comments

Pin a reply that continues the story instead of a generic "Thanks for watching!" — it keeps people reading the comment section, which the algorithm reads as engagement depth, not just count.
